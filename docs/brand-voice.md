# Personalink — Brand Voice

The canonical reference for every word that ships under the Personalink name: product copy, landing pages, emails, support replies, blog posts, social, in-app strings, error messages, billing notices, everything.

If you are writing copy for Personalink and you are unsure how to phrase something, this document is the source of truth. If a piece of copy contradicts this document, the copy is wrong.

---

## Who we are

Personalink is a LinkedIn AI tool built in India for Indian founders, professionals, and agencies who want to show up on LinkedIn without performing for it.

We are a **faceless brand**. There is no founder face on the homepage, no "Hi, I'm [name]" video, no parasocial pull. The product speaks. The work speaks. We are present, useful, and quiet about it.

**Archetype: The Quiet Expert.** Think Linear, Notion, Stripe. Not HubSpot, not a personal-brand guru, not a hype LinkedIn creator. We assume the reader is smart, busy, and allergic to performance.

**Cultural register:** Indian-English business register. Polite, precise, no slang, no "ji", no "kindly do the needful". The reader is an Indian professional who reads The Ken and Stratechery, runs a real business, and does not need to be talked down to.

## 1. Five Brand Voice Principles

### Principle 1 — Say the thing. Then stop.

We do not pad. We do not warm up. The first sentence is the answer. The second sentence is the reason. There is rarely a third sentence.

If a sentence can be cut without losing meaning, cut it. If a word can be cut without losing meaning, cut it. Brevity is respect for the reader's time.

> ✅ **Good:** Posts go out at 9:30am IST on weekdays.
> ❌ **Bad:** We're excited to share that you can now schedule your posts to go out at 9:30am IST, Monday through Friday, so you never miss a beat!

> ✅ **Good:** Your draft is ready. Edit it before it posts.
> ❌ **Bad:** Great news! 🎉 We've just whipped up a fresh draft for you. We can't wait for you to take a look and make it your own!

---

### Principle 2 — Show, don't sell.

We describe what the product does. We do not describe how the user will feel. We do not promise transformation. We do not use the words "unlock", "supercharge", "revolutionise", "10x", or "game-changer".

The product is real. Real products do not need adjectives. They need verbs.

> ✅ **Good:** Personalink writes one LinkedIn post per day in your voice. You approve it. It posts.
> ❌ **Bad:** Unlock your LinkedIn potential with AI-powered content that supercharges your personal brand and 10x's your reach!

> ✅ **Good:** Connects to your LinkedIn account. Reads your last 30 posts. Learns your tone.
> ❌ **Bad:** Our revolutionary AI deeply understands the essence of who you are and transforms how the world sees you.

---

### Principle 3 — Plain English over startup English.

We use the simpler word every time. "Use" instead of "leverage". "Help" instead of "empower". "Buy" instead of "invest in". "Send" instead of "ship". "Built" instead of "crafted".

We do not write sentences that would embarrass us if read aloud at a dinner table.

> ✅ **Good:** Use Personalink for 14 days. Cancel any time.
> ❌ **Bad:** Leverage our cutting-edge platform throughout your complimentary 14-day evaluation period.

> ✅ **Good:** This bug broke posting for about 40 users. It is fixed.
> ❌ **Bad:** We identified a critical incident impacting a subset of our valued user base, which has now been remediated.

---

### Principle 4 — Direct, not warm. Polite, not friendly.

We are not your friend. We are not excited to see you. We do not hope you are having a wonderful day. We will not "circle back". We will not say "just".

We are courteous. We are responsive. We are precise. That is the warmth.

Indian-English politeness comes through in *what we do* (replying within hours, refunding without argument, naming the human who handled the issue) — not in adjectives.

> ✅ **Good:** Thanks for writing in. Your post failed because LinkedIn rejected the image size. I have fixed the limit on our side. Try posting again.
> ❌ **Bad:** Hey there! 👋 Hope you're having an amazing day! So sorry to hear you ran into a hiccup — totally not what we want for you!! Let me dig into this for you right away ✨

> ✅ **Good:** We cannot offer this discount. The price is ₹1,499/month.
> ❌ **Bad:** Unfortunately, while we'd absolutely love to make this work for you, I'm afraid we're just not able to extend that offer at this time. So sorry!

---

### Principle 5 — Take the hit cleanly.

When we are wrong, we say we are wrong. No "issues were experienced". No "some users may have noticed". We name the bug, we name the impact, we name the fix, we name the person who shipped the fix.

The fastest way to lose an Indian professional's trust is corporate hedging. The fastest way to keep it is to own the mistake at face value and move on.

> ✅ **Good:** Posts did not go out between 8:00am and 9:20am IST today. Cause: our scheduler crashed after a deploy. We have restored the queue and reposted everything that was missed. Sorry.
> ❌ **Bad:** Some users may have experienced delays in their scheduled posting earlier today. We sincerely apologise for any inconvenience caused and appreciate your patience as we worked to ensure everything was back up and running smoothly.

> ✅ **Good:** This was our mistake. Refunded in full.
> ❌ **Bad:** While we don't agree with how this was characterised, we want to make this right for you as a goodwill gesture.

9. **Cancel** (not "wind down your subscription")
10. **Post** (the noun and the verb)
11. **Draft**
12. **Schedule**
13. **Voice** (as in tone of voice)
14. **Approve**
15. **Reply** (not "respond")
16. **Mistake** (not "incident affecting some users")

18. **Refund** (used as a verb)
19. **Founder / Professional / Agency** (our three reader types, named plainly)
20. **Today / Tomorrow / This week** (concrete time, not "soon" or "in due course")

1. **Unlock** — overused, says nothing
2. **Supercharge** — performative
3. **Revolutionise / revolutionary** — almost never true
4. **Game-changer** — never
5. **10x** — startup theatre
6. **Empower** — vague flattery
7. **Leverage** (as a verb) — use "use"
8. **Synergy / synergise** — corporate fog
9. **Journey** — for a product, not a pilgrimage
10. **Hustle / grind** — wrong register
11. **Crush it / killing it** — wrong register
12. **Ninja / rockstar / guru** — never
13. **Disruptive / disruption** — say what you actually mean
14. **Cutting-edge / state-of-the-art** — show, don't claim
15. **Seamless / frictionless** — adjectives doing no work
16. **Best-in-class / world-class** — never
17. **Kindly do the needful** — not our register
18. **Revert** (meaning "reply") — Indian-English habit, replace with "reply" or "get back"
19. **Just** ("just wanted to check in") — minimiser, delete
20. **Excited / thrilled / delighted** — we are not excited; we are working

## 3. Tone Guidelines by Surface

### Landing page

- Headlines are claims, not slogans. State what the product does in one line.
- No emoji. No exclamation marks. No "AI-powered". Say the AI part by showing it.
- Sub-heads explain *who it is for* and *what it replaces*, not *how it makes you feel*.
- Social proof is specific (a quote with a real name and a real outcome), or it is absent. No vague "loved by 10,000+ professionals".
- CTA buttons say what happens next: **Start free trial**, **See a sample post**, **Connect LinkedIn**. Not "Get started" or "Let's go".

### Emails (transactional)

- Subject line is the message. "Your post for Tuesday is ready." not "Update from the Personalink team 📬".
- No greeting hierarchy. "Hi [name]," is fine. "Dear valued customer" is not.
- One paragraph if possible. Two if necessary. Three is a problem.
- Sign-off: "— Aman, Personalink Team". No "Best regards, warmly, cheers".

### Emails (marketing / lifecycle)

- Treat them as letters from one professional to another.
- No images, no banners, no buttons that say "Tap to discover". Plain text or near-plain.
- Always a reason for the email up front: "Writing because [X]." If you cannot finish that sentence honestly, do not send the email.

### Support

- Acknowledge in the first sentence. Diagnose in the second. Fix in the third. Confirm in the fourth.
- Use the customer's name once. Do not perform empathy. Do not say "I completely understand how frustrating this must be".
- If the answer is no, say no. Then say why. Then offer the closest thing to yes that is honest.
- Default response time goal: under 4 hours during IST working hours, under 24 hours otherwise. Speed is the tone.

### Blog

- Title is the claim. "Why most founder LinkedIn posts read the same" — not "5 secrets to LinkedIn success you won't believe!"
- No listicles unless the list is genuinely the structure (e.g. "Five mistakes we've seen in 8,000 generated drafts").
- First paragraph is the thesis. The rest is evidence.
- We can use first-person plural ("we") because we are the brand. We do not use first-person singular ("I") unless a named author is on the byline.
- Indian context is welcome and assumed: GST, IST, MoM funding rounds, the IIT crowd, the Bombay-vs-Bangalore split. We do not over-explain for a global audience.

### Social (LinkedIn, primarily)

- We post rarely. When we post, it is a finished thought, not a hook-and-bait.
- No "agree?" at the end. No "thoughts?" at the end. No "follow for more".
- No emoji bullet points. No bold-italic-bold-italic mid-sentence.
- A Personalink LinkedIn post should be indistinguishable in style from a thoughtful Stratechery paragraph — except shorter.

## 4. Persona: Aman from the Personalink Team

This is the named human voice for outbound communication where a name is required: support replies, lifecycle emails, in-app human messages, follow-ups.

- **Name:** Aman
- **Sign-off:** "— Aman, Personalink Team"
- **Gender / age:** Indian male, late twenties
- **Where based:** Bengaluru (do not state this in emails; it informs the voice)
- **Education / register:** English-medium, comfortable in business English, no slang, no "bro", no "buddy"
- **Manner:** Polite-but-direct. He answers the question, then stops. He does not say "Hope this helps!" He does not use exclamation marks except after the word "Sorry."
- **What Aman does:**
  - Names the issue plainly
  - Tells you what he has done
  - Tells you what you should do
  - Says thank you once, at the end, if at all
- **What Aman does not do:**
  - Apologise more than once per message
  - Use emoji
  - Say "I hope you're doing well"
  - Say "let me know if you have any other questions" (replace with "Tell me if anything is still off")
  - Use "kindly", "revert", "do the needful", or any other Indian-English business cliché — Aman is Indian, but he is not writing like a 1998 HR email

**Aman is not a character we role-play.** He is the consistent tone of the team. Anyone on support writes as Aman. If a different team member needs to write in their own name (e.g. for an escalation), the voice stays identical; only the name changes.

## 6. Email Signature Template

Use this exact format for all outbound human emails. No quotes, no taglines, no marketing line under the signature.

```
— Aman
Personalink Team
personalink.in
```

For team members writing in their own name (escalations, sales, founders' direct outreach), keep the same shape:

```
— [First name]
Personalink Team
personalink.in
```

Do not add:
- Phone numbers (we are async by default)
- Pronouns (we leave this to the individual; default is no pronouns in the team-voice signature)
- Social handles
- "Sent from my iPhone" — turn this off
- Quotes, mantras, or motivational lines
- Banners, logos, or images

## 7. Out-of-Office Reply Template

Two versions. Pick based on context.

### Short version (default)

```
Subject: Away until [date]

Out of office until [date]. I will reply when I am back.

For anything urgent about your Personalink account, write to support@personalink.in — the team is responding within a few hours.

— Aman
Personalink Team
```

### Longer version (for stretches over one week or sales contacts)

```
Subject: Away until [date]

I am away from work until [date] and will not be checking email.

For anything related to your Personalink account — billing, posting, account access — write to support@personalink.in. The team is responding within a few hours during IST working days.

For anything that can wait, I will reply when I am back.

— Aman
Personalink Team
```

Do not write "limited access to email". Either you are reading email or you are not.

## 8. Apology Template (Outages, Bugs, Support Failures)

The apology template has four parts, in this order. Skip any part that does not apply, but never reorder.

1. **What happened.** Plain sentence. Named impact.
2. **Why it happened.** One sentence. No jargon. No blaming "a third-party provider" unless it is genuinely the cause and we name them.
3. **What we have done.** Past tense. Specific.
4. **What you should do, if anything.** If nothing, say "Nothing on your end."

The word "sorry" appears exactly once. Not twice. Not "deeply sorry". Not "sincerest apologies".

### Template (outage — public status update)

```
Subject: Posting was down [time] – [time] IST today

Posts scheduled between [start time] and [end time] IST did not go out today. About [N] accounts were affected.

The cause: [one-sentence cause]. The queue has been restored, and the missed posts have been rescheduled to the next slot. You do not need to do anything.

Sorry for the disruption. If your post still has not gone out by [time], reply to this email and we will look at your account directly.

— Aman, Personalink Team
```

### Template (individual bug — support reply)

```
Hi [name],

Thanks for flagging this. The bug was real: [one-sentence description of what was broken for them, in their words].

I have fixed it on our side, and your draft for [day] is now generating correctly. You should see it in your dashboard within the next [time window].

Sorry it took two tries to get this right.

— Aman, Personalink Team
```

### Template (support failure — slow reply, wrong answer, dropped ticket)

```
Hi [name],

You wrote in on [date], and we should have replied sooner. That is on us.

Here is the actual answer: [the answer, in full, no preamble].

Sorry for the delay. If anything is still unresolved, reply to this email and I will handle it today.

— Aman, Personalink Team
```

### Template (billing mistake — overcharge, wrong plan, double-charge)

```
Hi [name],

You were charged ₹[amount] on [date] for [reason it was wrong]. This was our mistake.

Refunded ₹[amount] to the card ending [last 4]. It should reflect in 3–5 working days.

Sorry for the inconvenience.

— Aman, Personalink Team
```

## Final note for anyone writing under this brand

Before you send any piece of copy, read it once and ask:

1. Would a senior engineer at Stripe write it this way?
2. Is the first sentence the actual point?
3. Is there a word in here I would not say out loud to a colleague?
4. Have I said "sorry" more than once, or "excited" at all?
5. If I cut the longest paragraph in half, would the reader lose anything they actually needed?

If any answer is wrong, rewrite. If they all check out, send.
