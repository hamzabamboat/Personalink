The wire hit our account at 11:47 PM on a Tuesday.

I refreshed the banking app seventeen times before I believed it was real.

₹4.2 crore. Our seed round. Closed.

What nobody tells you: the relief lasts about six hours. Then you remember you promised to 10x this money, and the weight of that sits differently than the weight of being broke.

We spent 11 months raising this round. Talked to 63 investors. Got 61 nos. The two yeses came from people who asked the hardest questions, not the easiest ones.

One of them made us redo our unit economics three times before the term sheet. I was annoyed then. I am grateful now.

The money changes some things. We can hire the senior backend engineer we have needed since January. We can run proper experiments instead of guessing. We can say no to bad-fit customers without panic.

It does not change the fact that we still have to build something people will pay for. The clock just got louder.

Three things I would tell someone starting this process:

Your deck matters less than you think. Your answers to "why now" and "why you" matter more.

Investors who ghost you are doing you a favor. The ones who say no with specifics are giving you free consulting.

The round closes when it closes, not when you need it to. Plan for twice as long as you think.

We are back to work tomorrow. The honeymoon is over.

What was the hardest part of your first fundraise?
